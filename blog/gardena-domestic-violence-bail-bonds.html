What Happens After a DV Arrest in Gardena

California Penal Code § 836(c) gives law enforcement officers the authority — and in many jurisdictions the obligation — to arrest when they have probable cause to believe a domestic violence offense occurred. Gardena PD officers respond to DV calls and will make an arrest if the evidence supports it, regardless of whether either party wants to proceed.

At the scene, the responding officer will issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) restricting contact between the defendant and the protected party for 5 to 7 days. The EPO is a civil order — it does not affect the defendant's right to bail, and bail can be posted as soon as booking is complete.

After the arrest, the defendant is transported to the Gardena Police Department jail for initial holding. Depending on the charges and available bed space, they may remain there briefly before being transferred to the Torrance Police Department Jail or, for longer holds and formal booking into the county system, to Twin Towers Correctional Facility in downtown Los Angeles.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take after a Gardena DV arrest?

When Gardena PD transfers a defendant to Twin Towers for county booking, total processing time from arrest to bail eligibility typically runs 6 to 10 hours. This includes the transfer, intake at Twin Towers, fingerprinting, criminal history check, and bail amount assignment. Weekend nights run slower. Once booking is complete, bail can be posted immediately.

Can bail be posted right after a domestic violence arrest in Gardena?

Yes. As soon as booking is complete and a bail amount appears on the LA County bail schedule, a bond can be posted. There is no mandatory waiting period or automatic hold for DV arrests. Angels Bail Bonds can begin the paperwork process as soon as your loved one appears in the county system — often while they are still being processed.

What is the 10% bail bond premium?

California law sets the bail bond premium at 10% of the total bail amount. This is the fee paid to the bail bondsman for posting the surety bond. It is non-refundable once the bond is posted. The advantage is that instead of posting $50,000 in cash, you pay $5,000 and the bondsman guarantees the full amount to the court.

Does the Emergency Protective Order block bail from being posted?

No. An EPO issued at the scene of a Gardena DV arrest is a civil no-contact order — it does not prevent bail from being posted or the defendant from being released. Once released, the defendant must follow all EPO terms. Violating the EPO while out on bail is a new criminal offense and can result in bail being revoked.
