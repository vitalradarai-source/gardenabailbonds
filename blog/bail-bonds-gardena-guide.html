LASD Torrance Station: Where Gardena Arrests Go

Gardena is primarily served by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) Torrance Station, located at 2675 Civic Center Dr, Torrance, CA 90505. When LASD deputies make an arrest in Gardena, the defendant is typically transported to this station for initial booking and holding.

For more serious felony charges or when the Torrance Station is at capacity, defendants may be transferred to one of the LA County central facilities — most commonly Men's Central Jail (MCJ) at 441 Bauchet St, Los Angeles or Twin Towers Correctional Facility at 450 Bauchet St. Transfers to central facilities extend the booking timeline significantly.

Gardena is incorporated as a city but LASD handles the majority of law enforcement. Some arrests by Gardena PD may be booked at the Gardena Police Department station before LASD transfer. We check all systems simultaneously when you call.

LA County Jail Booking Process — Step by Step

Step 1
Arrest and Transport
The arresting officer (LASD or Gardena PD) documents the charges and transports the defendant to the appropriate booking facility. All personal property is inventoried and stored.
Step 2
Live Scan Fingerprinting
Electronic fingerprints are run through state and federal databases via the Live Scan system. Outstanding warrants from other jurisdictions appear at this step. An active warrant from another California county can result in a hold that prevents immediate release.
Step 3
Photograph and Records Entry
Booking photos are taken and the arrest is entered into the LASD inmate management system. Public inmate searches through the LASD website typically reflect the booking 2 to 6 hours after it occurs at a station jail.
Step 4
Medical Screening
California law mandates a health screening for every new booking. Staff note medications, injuries, and medical needs. This step protects both the defendant and the facility, and can add time to the process for complex medical cases.
Step 5
Bail Determination
Bail is set according to the LA County Felony or Misdemeanor Bail Schedule, effective January 1, 2026. For most charges, bail is assigned at the schedule amount automatically. A judge can modify bail at arraignment.
Step 6
Cell Assignment and Phone Access
The defendant is housed and assigned to the jail phone system. This is typically the first opportunity for family contact. However, do not wait for that call — contact us now and we'll locate them first.

How Long Does Booking Take for a Gardena Arrest?

For misdemeanor arrests processed at the LASD Torrance Station, expect 4 to 8 hours from arrest to bail eligibility. Felony arrests that involve transfer to Men's Central Jail or Twin Towers can run 12 to 24 hours, and during high-volume weekend nights, even longer.

The key insight: you do not need to wait for the booking to complete to call us. We monitor the system and post bond the moment bail is set.

LA County Bail Schedule — 2026 Representative Amounts

The Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles publishes the Felony Bail Schedule and Misdemeanor Bail Schedule annually, effective January 1. These amounts are applied automatically at booking before arraignment. Under California Penal Code § 825, arraignment must occur within 48 hours of arrest (excluding Sundays and court holidays) — posting bail before arraignment prevents the judge from potentially raising the amount.

How a Bail Bond Works

A bail bond is a licensed surety instrument. Angels Bail Bonds posts the full bail amount as a guarantee to the court, and the defendant is released pending trial. Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the premium is 10% of total bail — non-negotiable by law. For a $30,000 bail, the premium is $3,000. Some clients qualify for a reduced 8% rate. We also offer flexible payment arrangements for qualifying clients.

The premium is earned when the bond is posted and is non-refundable regardless of how the case resolves. If the defendant appears at all required court dates, the bond is exonerated at case conclusion.
