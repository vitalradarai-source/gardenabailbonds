Where Gardena Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Gardena is transported to the Gardena Police Department Jail at 1718 West 162nd Street, Gardena, CA 90247 (phone: 310-217-9600). The Gardena Police Department operates a Type I detention facility at 1718 West 162nd Street. Gardena PD handles all municipal arrests with felony custody moving to the LA County jail system at Men's Central Jail or the IRC.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Los Angeles County Inmate Reception Center (IRC) at 450 Bauchet Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012. This is the intake point for the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) jail system, which also includes the Men's Central Jail in downtown LA and the Century Regional Detention Facility (CRDF) in Lynwood for female inmates. Gardena in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Gardena has its own police department and city jail at 1718 West 162nd Street, between the 110 and 405 freeways in South Bay LA County. GPD handles local bookings, and the jail is adequate for the initial booking phase. Overflow and felonies transfer to LA County IRC downtown.

Gardena sits at a transport crossroads — the 110 runs through the eastern edge, the 405 just west, and the 91 to the south. It is also one of the few California cities where licensed card rooms operate legally, which produces a specific arrest pattern around the Hustler Casino and the Normandie Casino properties. Intoxication calls, theft calls, and the occasional dispute spilling into the parking lot generate reliable police work there.

Beyond the casino corridor, Gardena has a significant industrial zone along Western Avenue and Rosecrans Avenue, and residential neighborhoods covering the rest of the city. GPD patrols are visible on Crenshaw Boulevard and Western Avenue. DUI enforcement is heavy on the 110/405/91 triangle. Misdemeanor bail at GPD is fastest; felonies move to IRC and the downtown processing delay kicks in.

How a Gardena Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Los Angeles County bail schedule — gardena bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at Gardena PD Jail and 4–8 hours at the Los Angeles County Inmate Reception Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Gardena arrest actually get processed?

Gardena arrests are processed at the GPD Jail at 1718 W 162nd Street. Misdemeanor bonds post here for fast release — often within 1–3 hours. Felonies transfer to LA County IRC at 441 Bauchet Street in downtown Los Angeles.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Gardena?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Gardena bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Los Angeles County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Gardena DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
